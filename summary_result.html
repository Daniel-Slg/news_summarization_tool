Friday Squid Blogging: New Extinct Species of Vampire Squid Discovered

Paleontologists have recently uncovered a 183-million-year-old species of vampire squid that lived in the shallows off an ancient island in what is now Europe. The researchers believe that the squid's exceptional preservation was due to unique oxygen-deprived conditions at the sea bottom where it suffocated, preventing scavengers from disturbing its remains. This remarkable find sheds light on the ancient marine life and provides valuable insight into the preservation of prehistoric creatures. A Cyber Insurance Backstop

In the first week of January, pharmaceutical giant Merck settled a lawsuit with its insurers over a $700 million claim filed after the NotPetya cyberattack in 2017. This raised debates on who should pay for state-sponsored cyberattacks, leading to discussions about a federal cyber insurance backstop. The idea is inspired by the Terrorism Risk Insurance Act and aims to provide financial support to insurers in the event of catastrophic cyberattacks. However, establishing clear criteria for triggering this backstop and defining what types of attacks it would cover pose significant challenges. Insurers are already limiting coverage for cyber risks, while courts are navigating complex exclusions related to cyberattacks involving nation-states. Some insurers are looking to exclude cyberattacks perpetrated by governments, leaving companies vulnerable and increasing the pressure on the government to provide assistance. But determining a backstop model that incentivizes strong cybersecurity practices poses difficulties due to a lack of empirical data about effective security controls. Without a solid understanding of what works in mitigating cyber risks, insurers and policymakers struggle to craft effective policies. Collecting and analyzing data on cybersecurity incidents is crucial for not only designing a cyber backstop but also for improving organizations' overall cybersecurity. Without this data-driven approach, the cybersecurity community risks being unable to adequately protect against large-scale cyberattacks, leaving victims to bear the brunt of the financial burden. #CyberInsurance #CyberSecurity #InsuranceIndustry #CyberRisk #FederalBackstop #NotPetya #DataCollection #CyberHygiene #PolicyChallenges #StateSponsoredAttacks

Apple Announces Post-Quantum Encryption Algorithms for iMessage

Apple has revealed its new post-quantum encryption standard, PQ3, based on the Kyber secure key-encapsulation protocol. This standard was chosen from the post-quantum algorithms selected by NIST in 2022. The detailed announcement can be found in Apple's blog post and Douglas Stabila's security analysis. AIs Hacking Websites

Recent research has revealed that large language models (LLMs) have advanced to the point where they can autonomously hack websites without human intervention. This capability includes tasks such as blind database schema extraction and SQL injections, even when the vulnerability is not known beforehand. The study highlights that models like GPT-4 can conduct such hacks due to their enhanced tool use capabilities and extended context understanding, but existing open-source models do not possess this ability. The findings raise concerns about the potential cybersecurity implications of deploying LLMs widely. #Cybersecurity #LLM #Hacking
